Hello, I'm Emmanuel Sifiso Nkosi

I’m a data scientist who didn’t take the traditional path not by choice, but by circumstance. What I lacked in opportunity, I made up for in grit, scholarships, and a relentless drive to prove I belonged in this space. My journey changed when a visionary manager at Telkom saw past the absence of a degree and chose to believe in my potential. She gave me the space to rise, and I made sure I never disappointed her or myself. I’m now proudly carving out my place in the tech world, and while I build tools, apps, and systems, I’m also building a future. A degree is still on my radar not for validation, but for the promise I made to myself, to keep going, no matter the pace, until I walk across that graduation stage.

My Journey

It was never easy and honestly, it was never promising. I didn’t have funding for varsity, and I watched opportunities slip past, one by one. But even without a clear path, I never let go of the vision I had for my future. I believed in the power of industry certifications and learnerships, and I used every chance I got to move forward. I started with an Information Technology learnership, where I developed interest in coding. That led to a Data Science scholarship funded by BCX, followed by an internship with them. When the programme ended, I was absorbed into a full-time role at the Telkom Corporate Centre as a Junior Data Scientist. That role pushed me further than I expected not just technically, but personally. It challenged me to lead interns on real-world projects like Acoustic Modeling and learning what it means to guide while still growing. Today, I’m a Data Science Analyst at Wits Enterprise based at University of the Witwatersrand. I’m part of a team focused on innovation across industries, I'm growing more than ever before, and I’m still building, still learning. Off the clock, you’ll probably find me where I’m most alive, on the soccer field, making music, gaming, or calling the next shot on a pool table.
